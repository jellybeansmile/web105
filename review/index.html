The Outsider Review

Summary

The Outsiders written by S.E. Hinton. It tells the story of 14-year-old named Ponyboy Curtis and his two older brothers, Soda and Darry. The boys are orphans and struggle to stick together in their lower-class neighborhood, known as the East Side. They and their friends are part of a gang of working class tough street boys called the Greasers.

At the age of 16 years old S.E. Hinton wrote about how revolutionized young adult fiction with its hard-hitting look at the social politics of being a teenager in mid-twentieth century America. It began a trend in the genre of taking a more serious look at the struggles of growing up.

About Ponyboy

Ponyboy Curtis, the youngest member of the greasers, being the main character of the novel. Ponyboy theorizes on the motivations and personalities of his friends and describes events in a slangy, youthful voice. Though only fourteen years old, he understands the way his social group functions and the role each group member plays. He sees that Two-Bit is the wisecracker, Darry the natural leader, and Dally the dangerous hood.

One of my favorite quotes from the book would have to be Stay gold, Ponyboy, stay gold.
